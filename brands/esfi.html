Operational Flow & League Execution

Coordinating national qualifiers for ESFI requires a highly structured operational workflow, as competitive fairness, broadcast timing, and lobby security are critical. My volunteering role spanned across the pre-event preparations and live matchday administrations for the national qualifiers in Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI) and FreeFire Max:

1

Tournament Prep & Event Roadmapping

Collaborated with core administrators to map out tournament stages, set up qualifier roadmaps, and configure the master competition calendar across weeks.

2

Roster Verification & Matchday Notification

Managed verified playing teams rosters, tracked captain check-ins, and established player communication loops by dispatching match notifications, timers, and lobby rules guidelines.

3

Lobby Administration & Live Hosting

Configured custom server rooms, invited team captains, verified in-lobby rosters against registered lists to prevent ringers/unauthorized players, and officiated on-time match launches.

4

Stat Processing & Broadcast Syncing

Monitored active gameplay to enforce tournament policies. Compiled rank points and kill counts at the end of each round, generating accurate live score sheets to feed the production broadcast overlays.

"Maintaining absolute tournament fairness under national broadcast spotlights." — Enforcing verified check-in procedures, resolving discord queries in real-time, and compiling stats post-match allowed for smooth broadcast integrations during high-stakes national qualifiers.

National Grind Tournaments

Detailed breakdown of the inaugural national championships where I administered tournament lobbies, verified player registration, compiled stats, and curated broadcast layouts:

BGMI National Grind (S1)

Sep 1, 2025 – Sep 14, 2025

The Electronic Sports Federation of India (ESFI) has successfully concluded the first major bracket of its inaugural National Grind – Season 1, featuring the widely popular title, Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI). The tournament, held from September 1st to 14th, witnessed massive participation from across the country, with over 300 teams and approximately 1,200 players competing for the championship title. The BGMI National Grind provided a crucial platform for both established and emerging esports talent to showcase their skills, strategic depth, and teamwork. The event’s structure, which progressed from open qualifiers to a high-stakes Grand Finale, ensured a rigorous and competitive environment, ultimately crowning the nation’s best team while highlighting the depth of India’s BGMI community.

Free Fire Max National Grind (S1)

Sep 19, 2025 – Oct 2, 2025

The Esports Federation of India (ESFI), the official government body for esports in India, has announced the Free Fire Max National Grind – Season 1, an online esports competition. The Free Fire Max National Grind – Season 1 tournament is scheduled to begin from September 2025 onwards. The tournament is open only to Indian players who are at least 16 years old, with registrations open from September 8 through September 15. I handled team communications, Discord ticket queues, and broadcast scoring setups to support the live execution.
