01 — The Project

The Brief

As an AI Filmmaker intern at Storyvord, I was tasked with creating a short AI cinematic sequence using an original idea. I chose the Fantasy genre and built "The Last Ember" — a story about a young temple keeper who carries the sacred flame through the night to a mountain summit, where dawn renews its power.

The goal wasn't just to generate beautiful images. It was to prove I could:

  • ✦ Maintain character consistency across 16 shots
  • ✦ Build a coherent visual world with cinematic lighting progression
  • ✦ Work within AI limitations and adapt my workflow in real time
  • ✦ Deliver a complete narrative with beginning, middle, and end

02 — The Process

How It Was Made

This project was built through an iterative collaboration between myself and Kimi (Moonshot AI), an AI assistant that guided the entire workflow — from story concept and character design to prompt engineering, shot-by-shot generation, and technical problem-solving.

Key decisions made together
◎

Simplified the story to one character, one lantern, one journey for maximum consistency

◎

Discovered that Seedance 1.5 Pro handles Chinese prompts better for video generation

◎

Identified that dolly-back camera motions cause AI hallucination outside the frame

◎

Adapted the narrative mid-workflow from "emergency rescue" to "annual ritual renewal" when the generated fire never looked dying

◎

Tested Start+End frame techniques for character continuity across shots

◎

Troubleshot ElevenLabs IP restrictions and pivoted to Luvvoice for voiceover production

This shows the reality of AI filmmaking: it's not about perfect tools. It's about critical thinking, rapid adaptation, and knowing when to change the plan.

04 — The Story

The Myth

Every year, the temple keeper must carry the sacred flame from the temple through the night to the mountain summit, where the first light of dawn renews its power. At sunrise, she returns to restore the strengthened flame to the temple brazier — a ritual that ensures the village's protection for another year.

语
Chinese Prompts for Higgsfield

Discovered that Seedance 1.5 Pro responds better to Chinese prompts for video generation. All video prompts were translated and optimized for the model.

Working With AI Limitations

✕
Dolly back / Pull back / Zoom out

AI hallucinates areas outside the original frame

→
✓
Removed all pull-back motions. Replaced with push-in, pan, or static camera moves.
✕
Face warping in close-ups

AI struggles with facial consistency during motion

→
✓
Kept face shots minimal in movement. Judged face first before keeping any clip.
✕
Hand/finger morphing

AI distorts fingers during complex hand motions

→
✓
Kept hands stable. Let camera and environment do the motion work.
✕
Body transition between poses

Start+End frame risky with large pose changes

→
✓
Used Start+End frames only for subtle transitions. Avoided kneeling or complex pose changes.
Mid-Workflow Adaptation

Originally planned an "emergency rescue" story — a dying flame that needed saving. But discovered that fire never looked dying in generated clips. Rather than forcing a broken narrative, I rewrote the story to an "annual ritual renewal" — matching the actual visual output. This shows critical thinking and professional judgment.
